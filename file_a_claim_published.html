How to File a Claim

File a new insurance claim through the InsurePortal Customer Portal by providing incident details, injured worker information, and supporting documentation.

Overview

Use this procedure when a workplace injury or incident has occurred and you need to submit a First Notice of Loss (FNOL) to initiate the claims process. Filing promptly ensures that the claim is registered, assigned to a handler, and processed within the required regulatory timeframe.

Claims filed through the portal are automatically linked to the relevant policy and routed to the assigned claims handler within 24 hours of submission.

Before You Begin

  • You are logged in to the InsurePortal Customer Portal.
  • You have an active policy number for the employer associated with the incident.
  • You have the date of injury and basic details of the incident.
  • Supporting documents are available (optional at submission but recommended):
    • Medical certificate or doctor's report
    • Incident or accident report
    • Witness statements (if applicable)

Steps

1

From the dashboard, click Quick Launch and select File a Claim.

Alternatively, navigate to Explore›Policies , select the relevant policy, and click File First Report of Injury from the policy actions bar.

2

On the Policy Information screen (Step 1 of 6), enter the Policy Number and select the Date of Injury using the date picker.

Tip: The policy number is printed on the policy certificate and in the My Policies section of the portal dashboard.

The system validates the policy number and confirms the employer name associated with the policy.
3

Click Continue to proceed to the Incident Details screen (Step 2 of 6).

4

Enter the incident details:

  1. Select the Time of Injury from the time picker.
  2. Select the Jurisdiction of Loss from the dropdown list.

    Note: The jurisdiction is typically the state or region where the incident occurred, not where the employer is based.

  3. Enter a brief description of the incident in the Incident Description field.
The form validates the jurisdiction selection and enables the Next button.
5

Click Next and enter the injured worker's details on the Injured Worker screen (Step 3 of 6):

  1. Enter the worker's First Name and Last Name.
  2. Enter the worker's Date of Birth.
  3. Enter the worker's Social Security Number (masked on screen).
6

Complete the Contact Information screen (Step 4 of 6) by entering the reporting person's name, phone number, and email address.

Note: Contact information is used by the claims handler to follow up if additional details are required. This does not have to be the injured worker.

7

On the Injury Details screen (Step 5 of 6), provide the nature of the injury:

  1. Select the Nature of Injury from the dropdown (for example, Fracture, Laceration, Burn).
  2. Select the Part of Body Affected.
  3. Select the Cause of Injury (for example, Fall from height, Machinery, Repetitive motion).
  4. Attach supporting documents by clicking Upload Document and selecting the relevant files.

    Tip: Supported formats: PDF, JPG, PNG, DOCX. Maximum 10 MB per file. Up to 5 documents.

8

On the Review and Submit screen (Step 6 of 6), review all entered information carefully.

To edit any section, click the Edit link next to the relevant section heading. You will be taken back to that step without losing data from other steps.

Important: Once submitted, the claim cannot be cancelled through the portal. Contact your claims handler if a submitted claim needs to be withdrawn.

9

Click Submit Claim to file the claim.

A confirmation screen displays the assigned Claim Number and the name of the Claims Handler. A confirmation email is sent to the contact email address provided in Step 6.

Result

The claim is filed and assigned a unique claim number. It is automatically linked to the associated policy and routed to a claims handler. You can track the claim status at any time by navigating to Explore›Claims and selecting the claim number.

Next Steps

  • Track the claim status to monitor progress and respond to any handler requests.
  • Upload additional supporting documents at any time from the Documents tab within the claim details view.
  • Contact your assigned claims handler directly using the contact details shown on the claim confirmation screen.
